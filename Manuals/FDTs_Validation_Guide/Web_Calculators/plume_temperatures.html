FDTs - Estimating Centerline Temperature of a Buoyant Fire Plume

  • Plume Temperatures
The plume centerline temperature is calculated using $$ \Delta T_p = \frac{9.1 \left( \frac{T_\infty}{g c_p^2 \rho_{a}^2} \right)^{1/3} \dot Q_c^{2/3}}{(z-z_0)^{5/3}} $$ The fire diameter is calculated using $$ D = \sqrt{\frac{4 A}{\pi}} $$ The virtual origin is calculated using $$ z_0 = -1.02 D + 0.083 \dot Q^{2/5} $$

Inputs

HRR (\(\dot Q\)): kW
Elevation above fire source (\(z\)): m
Area of combustible fuel (\(A_c\)): m\(^2\)
Convective heat release fraction (\(\chi_c\)): -

Ambient conditions:
Ambient temperature: 25 \(^{\circ}\)C
Specific heat of air: 1 kJ/(kg\(\cdot\)K)
Ambient air density: 1.18 kg/m\(^3\)
Acceleration of gravity: 9.81 m/s\(^2\)

Outputs

Convective HRR: kW
Fire diameter: m
Hypothetical virtual origin calculation: m
Mean flame height: m
Plume centerline temperature: \(^{\circ}\)C
